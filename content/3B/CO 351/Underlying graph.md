- The <u>underlying graph</u> of a digraph $D=(N,A)$ is obtained by removing the directions on the arcs in A to obtain edges.
![[Undirected and directed graphs-1746891875555.png|344x123]]
- A digraph is <u>connected</u> if its underlying graph is connected.
Note: a connected digraph might not have a dipath between certain ordered pairs of nodes,
- A <u>spanning tree</u> in a digraph =  A <u>spanning tree</u> in the underlying graph. ![[Undirected and directed graphs-1746893857156.png|154x137]]
- A <u>cycle</u> in a digraph = <u>cycle</u> in the underlying graph
