> [!definition] Cyclic groups
> A subset S of a group G **generates** G if $\langle S \rangle=G$
> A group G is **cyclic** if G=$\langle a \rangle\:for\:some\:a\in G$
- Example: 
	- $Z=\langle 1 \rangle=\langle -1 \rangle$ (generators are not unique)
	- $Z/n\mathbb{Z}=\langle [1] \rangle=\langle -[1] \rangle$
	- $\mathbb{Q}^{+}\:is\:not\:cyclic$
	- If G is a group, then $\langle a \rangle$ is a cyclic group for any $a\:\in G$ (cyclic subgroup generated by a)

> [!lemma] Lemma 2.6
> 1. If $a\in G,\:then\:\langle a \rangle=\{ a^{i}:\in \mathbb{Z} \}$
> 2. If $|a|=n,\:then\:\langle a \rangle=\{ a^{i}:0\leq i<n \}$

> [!proposition] Proposition 2.7
> If $G=\langle a \rangle$, then $|G|=|a|$
