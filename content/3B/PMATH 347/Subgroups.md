> [!definition] <u>Subgroup</u>:
>  let $(G,\cdot)$ be a group. A subset $H\subseteq G$ is a ***subgroup*** if 
> 1. For all $g,h\:\in\:H,g\cdot\:h\in H$ (H is **closed under products**) 
> 2. For all $g,h\in H,\:g^{-1}\in H$ (H is **closed under inverses**). And 
> 3. $e_{G}\in H$
> 4. Notation $H\leq G$

> [!Proposition] Proposition 2.1
>if H is a subgraph of (G, $\boxtimes$), then (H,$\boxtimes|_{H\times H}$) is a group such that
> 1.  The identity of H is $e_{H}=e_{G}$, and
> 2. The inverse of $g\in H$ is the same as the inverse of g in G

> [!proposition] Proposition 2.2
> H is subgroup **iff**
> 1. H is non-empty, and
> 2. $gh^{-1}\in H,\:\forall g,h\in H$

> [!proposition] Proposition 2.5
> - If $S\subset G,$ let $K=\{ e \}\cup \{ s_{1},\dots,s_{k}:k\geq1,s_{1},\dots,s_{k}\in S\cup S^{-1} \}$
> - Then $\langle S \rangle=K$

- **Subgroup generated by S in G is** $\langle S \rangle:=\bigcap_{S\subseteq H\leq G}H$ 
	- the smallest subgroup of a group G that contains all the elements of S.
- Example: 
	- if $G=(\mathbb{Z},+),\langle s \rangle=\{ 2 \}\text{ is the Cyclic Group of the set }\{ \dots,-6,-4,-2,0,2,4,\dots \}$ 
	- if $G=(\mathbb{R},+),\:then\:the\:subgroup\:\langle S \rangle=\{ 1,3 \}\:\text{generates the entire group }\mathbb{R}^{+}$
