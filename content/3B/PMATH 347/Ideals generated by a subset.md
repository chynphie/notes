> [!definition] Sum of Ideals  
> If $I, J \subseteq R$ are ideals, then $I + J := {x + y : x \in I, y \in J}$.

> [!corollary]  
> $(I \cup J) = I + J$ is the smallest ideal containing both $I$ and $J$.

**Proof.**  
By proposition, clearly $I + J \subseteq (I \cup J)$.  
For the reverse inclusion, suppose $s_i, t_i \in R$, $x_i \in I \cup J$ for $1 \le i \le k$.  
Let $S = {i : x_i \in I}$, so $\sum_{i \in S} s_i x_i t_i \in I$, and $\sum_{i \notin S} s_i x_i t_i \in J$.  
Then $\sum_{i=1}^k s_i x_i t_i = \sum_{i \in S} s_i x_i t_i + \sum_{i \notin S} s_i x_i t_i \in I + J$.

> [!note] Lattice of Ideals
> 
> - Ideals of $R$ are ordered by set inclusion $\subseteq$.
>     
> - The set of ideals of $R$ under this order forms the **lattice of ideals** of $R$.
>     
> - $R$ is the top element, $(0)$ is the bottom.
>     
> - Subgroup below $I_1, I_2$ in the lattice is $I_1 \cap I_2$.
>     
> - Subgroup above $I_1, I_2$ is $I_1 + I_2$.
>
