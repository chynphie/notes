> [!definition] Quotient groups
> Let $N\unlhd\:G$ . Then the setwise product $gN · hN = ghN$ makes $G /N$  
Into a group. Furthermore, the function $q : G → G /N : g \to gN$ is  
A surjective homomorphism with $ker q = N$.

Examples: 
- $\mathbb{Z}/5\mathbb{Z}$ in ($\mathbb{Z},+$) sends $a\:to\:a+5\mathbb{Z}$, which is $\mathbb{Z}_{5}$, works for all if 5 is an another number

> [!proposition] Normal subgroups are kernels
> Let $N \leq G$. Then there is a group $K$ and homomorphism
$φ : G \to K$ such that $N =kerφ$

> [!theorem] Universal property of quotients
> Suppose $\phi:G\to K$ is a homo. , and $N\unlhd\:G$. Let $q:G\to G/N$ be the quotient homo. .Then there is a homo. $ψ:G/N\to K$ s.t. $ψ\circ q= φ$ if and only if $N\:\subseteq\:ker φ$. Furthermore,if $ψ$ exists, then it is unique.

> [!theorem] First isomorphism theorem
> Suppose that $φ : G → K$ is a homomorphism. Then there is an isomorphism $ψ : G / ker φ → Im φ$ such that $φ = ψ \circ q$, where $q : G → G/ ker φ$ is the quotient homomorphism.
