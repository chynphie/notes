- A binary operation $\boxtimes$ : $X × X → X$ is **associative** if  $a\boxtimes(b\boxtimes\:c)= (a\boxtimes\:b)\boxtimes\:c$  for all $a, b, c ∈ X$
> [!proposition] Proposition
> A binary operation $: X × X → X$ is associative<u> if and only if</u> for all finite sequences $a_1, . . . , a_n ∈ X , n ≥ 1$, every bracketing of  $a_1, . . . , a_n$ evaluates to the same element of X
