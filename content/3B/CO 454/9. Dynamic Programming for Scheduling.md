- A **brute-force dynamic programming approach** to $1||\sum h_j$  
	  - Suppose $h_j$ is non-decreasing for every $j$.  
	  - Let $J$ denote a subset of the jobs $\{1, 2, \dots, n\}$.

- Define:
	  - $P(J) := \sum_{j \in J} p_j$
	  - $H(J) :=$ minimum total penalty for the **subproblem** that only considers $j \in J$  (assume every $j \in J$ comes after all jobs in $J \setminus \{j\}$)

- **Recursion**:  
	  $$H(J) = \min_{j \in J} \left\{ h_j(P(J)) + H(J \setminus \{j\}) \right\}, \quad H(\emptyset) = 0
  $$
- Apply the recursion idea to $1||\sum T_j$:
$$
V(J) := \min \sum T_j \text{ when the set of jobs is } J
$$
#### Dynamic Programming Stages
- **Stage 0**: $|J| = 0$  
  $$
  V(\emptyset) = 0
  $$

- **Stage 1**: $|J| = 1$  
  $$
  V(J) = V(\{j\}) = \max \{0, p_j - d_j\} \quad \forall j \in \{1, \dots, n\}
  $$

- $\cdots$

- **Stage $s$**: $|J| = s$  
  $$
  V(J) = \min_{j \in J} \left\{ V(J \setminus \{k\}) + \max \left\{ 0,\ \sum_{j \in J} p_k - d_k \right\} \right\} \quad \forall J \subseteq \{1, \dots, n\},\ |J| = s
  $$


> [!theorem] Theorem 51
> There exists an optimal schedule for $1||T$ in which job i comes before job j for every pair of jobs i,j s.t. $p_{i}\leq p_{j}$ and $d_{i}\leq d_{j}$
